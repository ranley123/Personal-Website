About Me

Why be afraid of infinity of truth? Joy resides in every step of growth.

My interests in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Machine Learning derived from a very young age, when I was often wondered if a series of seeming chaos could ultimately converge to one line of concise description or rule. Or, we could use a rule as a "skeleton" to restore the original development trend of events.

For this reason, I chose science and engineering as my subject group in high school. Meanwhile, I read many books about ancient Chinese philosophers and their ideas. They firmly believed that everything can be mathematical abstraction and human are able to master the nature of development of things, where the essence is the combination of factors with their weights continually varying. Their beliefs deeply influenced my outlook on the world and values, and I made up my mind to find the answer for it.

I realized that my question can be studied in a link between human and computer. By extracting the high-level pattern of human behaviors or thoughts into programmable and mathematical concepts, computer can proactively speculate customizable operations based on preferences of individuals. This aspect shows the convergence of chaos. 3-D reconstruction also fascinates me, for it demonstrates the reverse process from the "skeleton", the original object.
